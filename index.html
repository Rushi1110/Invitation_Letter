India, Tennis & Roger Federer: 

Tennis is rampantly followed in India, it being one of the highest contributors to tennis match streamings across the globe. However, it is yet to take its rightful place when it comes to participating and performing on a world stage. The primary reason for that is not the lack of world class amenities but the lack of real motivation that sports can be a career too. 

 

Roger Federer has been a continual inspiration to the youth of the world, with the biggest fan following base from India. Most of us have grown up watching him play and have idolised his way of life which extends beyond sports. His active involvement in promoting tennis in third world countries and his pioneering work in uplifting the underprivileged children's education is what makes him the perfect fit for our festival.

About WIN India

WIN India is an organisation that started when COVID-19 first hit India and the whole country was pushed into a lockdown.  Top  colleges across India , including institutes of eminence like the IITs, IIMs and NITs, came together with an aim to inculcate a healthy living amongst the youth of the country in face of the pandemic. 

 

WIN India 2.0 aims to take this agenda even further and unite the students of the nation under the banner of sports, while simultaneously solving real challenges faced by the India today- the biggest being education for the underprivileged children of rural India.  

 

Our festival acts as a fundraiser through sporting activities to help provide the necessary resources to support the infrastructure for children's education.
